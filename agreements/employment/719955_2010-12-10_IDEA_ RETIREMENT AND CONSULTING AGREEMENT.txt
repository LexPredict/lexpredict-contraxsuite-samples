&lt;p style="margin-top: 12px; margin-bottom: 0px;"&gt;&lt;font style="font-family: Times New Roman;" class="_mt" size="2"&gt;On January&amp;nbsp;25, 2010, the independent members of the Board of Directors (the "Board") of Williams-Sonoma, Inc. (the "Company") approved the Company's entry into a Retirement and Consulting Agreement (the "Agreement") with W. Howard Lester, the Company's former Chairman and Chief Executive Officer.&amp;nbsp;Pursuant to the terms of the Agreement, Mr.&amp;nbsp;Lester retired as Chairman and Chief Executive Officer and as a member of the Board on May&amp;nbsp;26, 2010. Upon his retirement and in recognition of his contributions to the Company, Mr.&amp;nbsp;Lester received, among other things, accelerated vesting of his outstanding stock options, stock-settled stock appreciation rights and restricted stock units. The total expense recorded in the thirty-nine weeks ended October&amp;nbsp;31, 2010 associated with Mr.&amp;nbsp;Lester's retirement, consisting primarily of stock-based
compensation expense, was approximately $4,319,000. As a result of Mr.&amp;nbsp;Lester's death on November&amp;nbsp;15, 2010, the Agreement terminated and all unvested stock units and cash payments granted under the Agreement were forfeited.&lt;/font&gt;&lt;/p&gt; &lt;/div&gt;</NonNumbericText><NonNumericTextHeader>NOTE H:&amp;nbsp;RETIREMENT AND CONSULTING AGREEMENT
